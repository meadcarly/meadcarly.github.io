On a Personal Level

Hi there!

My name is Carly and I am a burgeoning software developer! I know I am new to the game, but when I decided to change careers, I realized that I was not starting over. In fact, I am bringing some pretty powerful skills to the table, even before I begin coding. You see, before this I was a nurse for 13 years and that taught me some valuable things. I won't try to enumerate through all of them, but let me talk about a few.

  1. I am an organized worker who is able to prioritize, set goals, and get projects done in a timely fashion. While this is absolutely essential as a nurse, it's a welcome skill base in every other career as well.
  2. I am well versed in collaboration, have effective communication skills, and I value everyone's insight. These skills make me a great addition to any team.
  3. I am willing to make mistakes. I don't like to, don't get me wrong, but I find that getting in there and trying, making mistakes, and figuring out how to fix them is really the best way to learn. It also helps you grasp the full picture, which makes the job easier in the long run.

I was in my last position for 10 years. I started with limited experience in that particular field, but I worked hard and I was always asking "why". By the time I decided I was ready for a change, I had co-written an entire orientation manual for new hires, not only for my job but also for the position I suprvised. I am so grateful I was given a chance to build on my skills, and I know I am worth investing in as I transition to this new career.
